Portfolio Rosa Amptmeijer

Enschede firework disaster installation

Summary

For my graduation project, I developed an interactive installation for a museum. I worked with MuseumFabriek in Enschede and created an installation about the Enschede fireworks disaster to browse interviews.

Context

Initially, the exhibit consisted of a compilation of five looped videos. Visitors could watch them but had no choice in what they saw. My goal was to make the experience interactive, let visitors explore what interested them, let them learn more about specific topics, and understand the relationships between different people involved. Adding interactivity made the experience both more engaging and educational.

The installation during the testing phase in the museum

Process

I began with literature research to define the right framework and requirements. Five key design principles emerged:

The installation must address multiple audiences
It must allow free exploration.
It must support multiple users simultaneously.
It must provide added value beyond static displays.
Language should remain accessible (B1 Dutch level).


I brainstormed by mapping inputs/outputs and creating word maps. After explorative stakeholder interviews, I sketched four concepts that were validated with the stakeholder. The museum chose the concept of an interactive installation that uses the interviews displayed in the existing exhibit. I analyzed those interviews, identified a clear timeline and four themes, and designed an interface where visitors could choose a theme and time, triggering the corresponding video clip. The installation was made with Unity.
After testing the installation in the museum, conclusion were made that a lot of the interations were unclear. A new iteration with significant changes was made so it better guided the user thoughout using the application.

Results

The final prototype after the redesign

The installation was placed in the musuem, it was tested during a 2 month period. behaviour was measured by combining qualitative and quantitative data from log data, direct observation, questionnaires (based on the Museum Experience Scale), and interviews. From these results, a new iteration was made. This redesign makes the options for the user a lot clearer and shows a preview of the video they are about to watch. This redesign stayed as a wireframe in Figma and isn't developed further.

Reflection

Although the project had challenges, including its size and my other commitments such as local politics, it was highly educational. I gained hands-on experience with Unity and Figma, and I learned how to translate design processes into real-world solutions. Personally, I grew in identifying my own pitfalls, managing long-term projects, and working under stress. Going through this process, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, taught me to stay on the goal and not get distracted by unnecessary details.
